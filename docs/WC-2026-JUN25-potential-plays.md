## Match Rationale

### Curacao vs Ivory Coast

The Ivory Coast team total has direct independent support, while the corner market
captures territorial pressure without requiring clinical finishing. Curacao's 5-4-1
and Eloy Room's recent 15-save performance are the primary risks. Action Network's
halftime-draw thesis also warns against paying for an automatic early avalanche.

### Ecuador vs Germany

This is the least settled matchup. RotoWire supports under 2.5 because Ecuador has not
scored and Germany has already clinched; Action Network instead supports BTTS because
Ecuador must open up. The under remains only a lineup-sensitive potential play.
Germany's moneyline is the simpler secondary expression of its depth advantage.

### Japan vs Sweden

Action Network and the New York Post both support a higher-scoring match. Sweden must
win and is expected to pair Isak with Gyokeres, while Japan can counter into the space.
RotoWire separately supports over 9.5 corners, represented by Kalshi's 10+ contract.

### Tunisia vs Netherlands

Tunisia has lost twice by four goals and conceded nine total. Independent previews
support Netherlands -2.5, while FanDuel identifies Gakpo as the scorer most directly
connected to a projected 4-0 result. Remove the scorer candidate if Gakpo does not start.

### Turkiye vs USA

BTTS has broad analyst support because the USA is expected to rotate and Turkiye has
generated 61 shots without scoring. Buying No on the USA winner contract is the Kalshi
equivalent of Turkiye double chance. That secondary candidate depends on the expected
USA rests actually occurring.

### Paraguay vs Australia

This is the strongest incentive-based low-event script. A draw works directly for
Australia and should leave Paraguay on a qualifying four points. Action Network selects
BTTS No, while RotoWire supports the draw. Miguel Almiron's suspension further reduces
Paraguay's creation.
